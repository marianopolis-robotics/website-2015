Tutorial: Introduction to PID

We will be using Vex parts and EasyC V4 for Cortex in this tutorial. EasyC V4 for Cortex has a built-in PID Control function block.

In order to set up PID control, you will need a Vex integrated motor encoder (IME). What is an IME? The Vex IME is an add-on for your Vex motors. You can follow Vex instructions to install an IME onto your Vex motors. Without an IME, you are programming your robot to turn a motor a certain number of clicks (rotation units). With a programmed IME, you can make your motor complete a specific number of rotations or keep something held at a certain angle.

What is PID control? It stands for Proportional-Integral-Derivative. You may not know some of these words if you haven't done Calculus; you do not have to worry, because we will come up with easy-to-understand explanations for all of these. P.I.D. control is a “control algorithm” which controls what a motor is doing by making small adjustments thanks to information that the encoder is inputting.

Proportional: the proportional part is what gets your motor going to your target. You might go over your target (or under your target), but at least you are around where you want to be. Think of a driver trying to get her car to a speed of 50 kilometers per hour: it doesn't matter too much if she's travelling at 48, 49, 51 or 52 so long as the car is around its target.

Integral: the integral part takes into account how “far away” under your target speed your motor is rotating and tries to adjust for the difference. For example, if you were lifting something up thanks to a motor but gravity was pulling it down and making the motor's job more difficult, the P.I.D. control would make the motor spin faster.

Derivative: the derivative part tries to avoid making the motor go over the target. It thinks about what is going to happen using how quickly the motor is speeding up as determined by the proportional part.

We hope you found this introduction to PID control helpful. PID control can be very helpful and is widely used in industrial production as well as in robots.
